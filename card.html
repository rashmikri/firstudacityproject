German shephard

The German Shepherd Dog is one of America’s most popular dog breeds — for good reason. He’s an intelligent and capable working dog. His devotion and courage are unmatched. And he’s amazingly versatile, excelling at most anything he’s trained to do: guide and assistance work for the handicapped, police and military service, herding, search and rescue, drug detection, competitive obedience and, last but not least, faithful companion.

  • Scientific Name: Canis lupus familiaris
  • Average Length: 26 centimeters
  • Average Lifespan: 14 years
  • Habitat: Tropical rain forest in southeastern Brazil

German Shepherds are described as a breed that has an “eagerness to have a purpose.” This is why German Shepherds make such amazing working dogs such as police aids, disability assistance, search-and-rescue work, and military roles. Part of your German Shepherd puppy’s purpose will be to please you and and make sure your are safe. German Shepherds tend to be “one family” pets, as in they can be suspicious of strangers. They will want to please you 24/7!
